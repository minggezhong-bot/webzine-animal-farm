Dorm Garden Diary

Jan 16

  • New Haven winter feels long and gray
  • I miss sunlight
  • On my birthday a friend gives me a small countertop garden
  • I place it in my dorm like a tiny window
  • I fill the water
  • I turn on the light
  • I feel better right away
Jan 18

  • I plant chives cilantro dill and parsley
  • It feels like planting future meals
  • I keep checking the pods
  • I talk to the seeds like they can hear me
  • I tell them please grow
  • Outside is cold
  • Inside is warm and pink from the lamp
Jan 21

  • The first sprout appears
  • It is very small
  • But it is real
  • I take a photo
  • I feel proud for no reason
  • I start checking the garden too often
  • I watch every tiny change
  • It becomes my daily routine
Jan 24

  • Chives grow straight and calm
  • Dill grows soft and wild
  • Parsley and cilantro take their time
  • They look shy at first
  • The light makes everything look like a little stage
  • My dorm corner becomes greener
  • I feel less stuck in winter
Jan 29

  • The plants get taller
  • Dill starts taking too much space
  • I trim a little
  • I feel guilty for one second
  • Then I smell the fresh herbs
  • I put them in food
  • Suddenly my dinner feels brighter
Feb 1

  • I start a side project
  • Mung bean sprouts
  • I soak the beans
  • I rinse them
  • I cover them
  • I repeat
  • A few days later they explode into a dense messy pile
  • Some look pale yellow
  • But they are crisp and alive
  • I feel like I made a small miracle in a bowl
Feb 5

  • I add basil and mint
  • Now I have two systems
  • The machine is stable and neat
  • The pots feel freer and more unpredictable
  • Every morning I check water and leaves
  • It is a simple kind of care
  • It makes my day start softer
Feb 10

  • Outside winter is still winter
  • But inside I have my own season
  • I watch new leaves appear
  • I watch stems reach up
  • I cut herbs and use them
  • I do not need a big garden
  • I just need a small green proof that time is moving
Feb 16

  • More light helps
  • Too much crowding makes them weak
  • A little trimming is kind
  • Fresh herbs change simple food
  • The best part is not the harvest
  • The best part is the daily looking
Feb 23

  • This is not just about plants
  • It is a winter plan
  • When the sky is dim I turn on my own sun
  • When I miss nature I grow a small piece of it
  • When days feel the same I watch something slowly change
